# Narrative Timeline: Alice's Adventures in Wonderland

## Chapter 1 — Down the Rabbit-Hole

### Core events
- Alice, bored by a book without pictures or conversations, follows a waistcoat-wearing White Rabbit.
- She falls through a seemingly endless shaft lined with cupboards and curiosities.
- She enters a hall of locked doors and discovers a tiny key and a miniature door leading to a beautiful garden.
- A bottle labeled “DRINK ME” triggers her first dramatic size change.

### Retrieval anchors
- Curiosity as inciting force.
- Threshold imagery: corridor, key, door, bottle.
- First identity destabilization through physical transformation.

## Chapter 2 — The Pool of Tears

### Core events
- Alice’s size instability escalates: she grows too large, then shrinks.
- She cries enough tears to create a literal pool.
- She begins to question who she is, reciting lessons incorrectly.

### Retrieval anchors
- Memory failure as identity crisis.
- Child cognition under surreal stress.
- Body change + emotional overflow connection.

## Chapter 3 — A Caucus-Race and a Long Tale

### Core events
- Alice and animals become wet and seek a way to dry off.
- Dodo organizes a nonsensical caucus-race with no clear start or finish.
- Arbitrary authority resolves the race: everyone has won.

### Retrieval anchors
- Satire of procedure without logic.
- Social ritual replacing reason.
- Wordplay and misunderstandings in multi-species dialogue.

## Chapter 4 — The Rabbit Sends in a Little Bill

### Core events
- White Rabbit mistakes Alice for his servant and orders her around.
- Alice grows gigantic inside Rabbit’s house after eating.
- Chaos erupts as animals try to remove her; she later shrinks and escapes.

### Retrieval anchors
- Status confusion and class satire.
- Agency fluctuation: Alice as both subordinate and threat.
- Food-mediated transformations as plot motor.

## Chapter 5 — Advice from a Caterpillar

### Core events
- Caterpillar questions Alice repeatedly: “Who are you?”
- Alice struggles to answer coherently due to constant changes.
- Mushroom instructions allow targeted growth/shrinking control.

### Retrieval anchors
- Identity as a rhetorical problem.
- Ambiguous mentorship.
- First move from accidental to strategic transformation.

## Chapter 6 — Pig and Pepper

### Core events
- Duchess’s household is chaotic and violent; pepper overwhelms the environment.
- A baby transforms into a pig.
- Alice meets Cheshire Cat, who frames all Wonderland inhabitants as mad.

### Retrieval anchors
- Domestic space as absurd theater.
- Caregiving parody.
- Madness framed as universal baseline.

## Chapter 7 — A Mad Tea-Party

### Core events
- Alice joins March Hare, Hatter, and Dormouse in circular, hostile conversation.
- Time is personified and reportedly offended, resulting in perpetual tea-time.
- Riddles and stories collapse into unresolved logic games.

### Retrieval anchors
- Temporal breakdown.
- Conversation as social gatekeeping.
- Nonsense as power performance.

## Chapter 8 — The Queen’s Croquet-Ground

### Core events
- Alice enters the Queen’s domain: volatile justice, frequent death sentences.
- Croquet is played with flamingos as mallets and hedgehogs as balls.
- Cheshire Cat’s partial presence causes jurisdictional confusion.

### Retrieval anchors
- Arbitrary sovereign violence.
- Rules changing mid-game.
- Embodied absurdity in sport and law.

## Chapter 9 — The Mock Turtle’s Story

### Core events
- Gryphon escorts Alice to Mock Turtle.
- Mock Turtle recounts schooling and nostalgic suffering in pun-heavy language.
- “Reeling and Writhing” parodies curriculum and educational legitimacy.

### Retrieval anchors
- Satire of institutional education.
- Trauma narrated as performance.
- Puns as epistemic distortion.

## Chapter 10 — The Lobster Quadrille

### Core events
- Mock Turtle and Gryphon explain/demonstrate dance rituals.
- Narrative shifts between dance instruction, song, and anecdote.
- Formal patterning masks incoherent significance.

### Retrieval anchors
- Ritual form vs. semantic emptiness.
- Social belonging through confusing etiquette.
- Performance as meaning substitute.

## Chapter 11 — Who Stole the Tarts?

### Core events
- Trial begins over stolen tarts; Knave of Hearts is accused.
- Court procedure is visibly unjust and absurd.
- Evidence handling is theatrical rather than rational.

### Retrieval anchors
- Legal parody.
- Burden of proof inverted/ignored.
- Spectacle over truth.

## Chapter 12 — Alice’s Evidence

### Core events
- Alice grows in confidence and literal size during the trial.
- She challenges nonsense testimony and authority structures.
- Wonderland court collapses into chaos; dream frame breaks.

### Retrieval anchors
- Moral maturation through resistance.
- Speech as reclamation of agency.
- Ending ambiguity: dream logic and waking critique.

## Cross-chapter progression summary

1. **Curiosity → Disorientation** (Ch. 1–2)
2. **Disorientation → Adaptive navigation** (Ch. 3–6)
3. **Adaptive navigation → Institutional critique** (Ch. 7–11)
4. **Institutional critique → Self-assertion** (Ch. 12)
